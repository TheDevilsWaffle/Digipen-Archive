Mehen

Mehen, or coiled Snake games are some of the oldest Egyptian race games. The playing pieces, tiny lions and small balls, were moved from the tail of the snake to the goal on its head.

  • Goal: Be the first player to reach the final space with all of your tokens.
  • Mechanics: Race, Token Exchange, Resource Management
  • Components: 3d2, 6 Pawns/Player, Chips, Board

Snakes & Ladders

Originated in ancient India, most likely based on the earlier Coiled Snake games. The game was a morality game teaching that good acts lead you close to nirvana. The earliest boards were circle based. In 1893, R.H. Harte introduced the square board varient to England.

The Royal Game of Goose

The Game of Goose, like Snakes & Ladders, is all about life’s ups-and-downs. Exact origins of the game are unknown, the oldest written info about the game dates back to the 16th Century. The game was recognized as arriving in England on June 16th, 1597 from an inscription in the register of Stationer’s Hall in London.

War Games

Chaturanga

Father of Chess, Chaturanga means guadripartite (divided into four parts). It was used to descibe an Indian army from Vedic times, which had four parts: 1 elephant, 1 chariot, 3 cavalry, and 5 foot soldiers. The board was called the ASHTAPADA(eight square). Its pieces are very similar to Chess: Raja/King, Mantri/Counselor/Queen, Gaja/Elephant/Bishiop, Asva/Knight, Ratha/Chariot/Rook, and Pedati/Infantry/Pawns.

Shogi

Shogi is played on a 9x9 square board. Pieces promote on the opposite third of the board. All pieces promote to Gold General except as noted. Captured pieces may be dropped as your own as your turn, once per turn. The pieces are:

  • King: like a normal king
  • Rook: moves like normal rook, promotes to Dragon King, which gains ability to move 1 space diagonal
  • Bishop: moves as normal bishop, promotes to Dragon Horse, which gains ability to move 1 space orthagonally
  • Gold General
  • Silver General
  • Knight
  • Lance
  • Pawn

Xiangqi

Game is played on a board of 9x10 lines with the pieces played on the points, or intersections of the line. The center of the board is separated by the river, which Elephants may not cross.

  • General: move one space orthogonally, must stay within palace
  • Mandarin: move one space diagonally, must stay within palace
  • Elephants: move two spaces diagonally, must stay on their side of the river
  • Horses: move as Knight in Chess, but intervening space must be empty
  • Soldiers: move one space forward until crossing the river, where it gains ability to move sideways
  • Chariots: move as Rook in Chess
  • Cannons: move as a Chariot, but must jump over another piece to capture

Maharajah & The Sepoys

A variant from India in the 19th Century. One player has a full set of pieces, where the other has only the Maharajah, which can move like a Queen and Knight combined.

Gala (Farmer’s Chess)

A variant from Schleswig-Holstein, Germany. Object is to capture both enemy Kings (no check). Central cross zone causes pieces to deflect (rooks and bishops trade movement roles in this zone).

Courier Game

Appeared in Germany in the early 13th Century, and was played for several centuries. Probable source of the modern Bishop. Featured a enlarged board, Queen moves 1 space diagonally or 3 on the first move, Bishop moves 2 squares diagonally and jumps, Courier that moves like a modern Bishop, Jester, that moves 1 space orthogonally, Sage that moves like a King, and a double steop rule for the King's and Queen's Pawns.

Circular/Byzantine Chess

Played as early as 10th Century in Persia. Played with the same pieces as Shantranj. Variant called Citadel for each side in the center of the board. Reaching an opponent’s Citadel with your King forces a draw.

Stratego

Often cited as a modern game derived from Chess, but with imperfect information. Stratego is a licensed cariant derived from L’attaque, published in Europe in 1910 by Jumbo, designed by Mdme. Hermance Edan in 1908. Milton Bradley licensed and published the game as Stratego in 1961.

Design Principles

  • Chess is a game of perfect information
  • Chess is a game with a goal of player elimination through a mechanic of deterministic attrition

Wei-Ch’i or Go

Origins of Wei-Ch’i date to Chinese writings dating from 635 (AD or BCE?). First known books on the game from 618 – 906. Arrived in Japan around 500 BCE. By the end of the first millennium commoners were playing the game in Japan. State school founded in a6th Century—first professional gamers! Go espouses estehtics throughout the game.

Go is all about territory. The goal is to capture territory by occupying the intersections of two lines, so that adjacent line combinations create an area of adjacent stones.

Go is layed on a 19 x 19 board, although it is sometimes practiced on a 9 x 9 board.

Go is the root for Othllo (a.k.a. Reversi) and Blokus (game of the year for 1999).

Gomoku

Gomoku (or Renju or Gobang) is Japanese for "five in a row". Generally considered a children's game in Asia (as opposed to Go). Gomok means "five pieces". It is traditionaly played on a Go board with Go stones. The goal is to be the first to get 5 in a row horizontally, vertically, or diagonally. Rows of more than 5 (called overlines) do not count. Players are not permitted to create open-ended forks of 3 stones each. Three and four or four and four are allowed, however. It is a lot lie Tic-Tac-Toe or Connect Four.

Pente

Pente is an adapttion of Gomoku by Gary Gabrel in 1977. Pente was acquired by Parker Brothers in 1983. The main difference in the game is that it allows capturing pairs of stones. Caprturing 5 pairs is an alternate win condition.

Morris Games

Morris games were found at the Temple at Kurna, Egypt, as the incomplete Alquerque board from 1400 BCE. The game had made its way to China as Luk tsut K’ic by 500 CE. Games were disconvered in Bronze Age Ireland. In all versions of the game, players take turns adding stones to the board.

Dominoes

Dominoes first came to Europe through Italy in the early 18th Century. They lost their series designation, added blanks, and removed duplicates. Probably named for the balck and white domino mask worn in Venetian Carnical, which in turn was named for the black hood with white interior worn by French clergy during winter. Dominoes are traditionally made of bone, ivory, or wood. Thus, the draw pile is often called the “woodpile” or “boneyard”.

Mahjong

Around 1610, the Chinese document Yeh-tzu describes Matiao, an almost cetain direct ancestor of the game Mahjong. Matiao was a trick-taking game for four players, played with four-suited money cards. THree of the four matiao suits resemble the three suits of the mah-jongg. Coins correspond to Bams; Coin Strings Myriads correspond to Craks. The deck composition and game play is quite diferent. The game first came into general wareness around the late 1880’s.

There are three origin theories:

  • Mahjong probably originated in the provinces near Shanghai because no reconds of mahjong are found in any other part of China before 1900.
  • A rival theory credits one or more inventors in Ningpo with the act of transposing playing cards used for the game of Matiao onto ivory and banboo about 1870 or 1880 as this is the area where playing cards were first recreated in ivory tablets.

The Rise & Fall of Mahjong

In 1920 Joseph Park Babcock introduced the game to the US in 1920, when he renamed it to Mah-Jongg and published a book that simplified and standarized the rules, ules of Mah-Jongg. In 1922, Parker Brothers tried a test market of the game, but it had disappointing results. In 1923, seeing the fad pick up, Parker Brothers started selling sets from $2.50 to $150. By 1922, ranked 6th in value of exports from Shanghai at $1,505,00 (equivilent to $14.3 Million). In 1923, the Department of Commerc reported that large quantities of bone were being exported from Kansas City and Chicago to China in order to turn out more sets. In 1924 the fad began to cool, and by 1929 the game had almost entirely faded from public awareness.
