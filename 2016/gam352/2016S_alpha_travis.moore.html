PLAYTESTING GOAL:
The purpose of this playtest was to test the current design of the HUD to see if it matched the feel of the game in style, was intuitive/understandable, and to get any other opinions that playtesters are willing to share.


PLAYTESTER 1:
Ian Aemmer

PLAYTEST LENGTH:
15 Minutes

NOTES:
Immediately Ian commented that the HUD looked rather large and took up quite a lot of screen space. At the time our HUD was using primarily white on the background and the borders. Ian thought that HUD took too much attention away from the game due to the white color and the size of the HUD. Ian understood what all sections of the HUD were meant to represent except for the pest counter and the jump meter. Ian suggested different colors for each player's HUD which sounds like a great idea. Ian wants our HUD to have sound effects and animations when actions in game affect values on the HUD (these features have not been implemented yet). Ian did not comment on how the HUD matched the look of our game.

PLAYTESTER 2:
Stephen Gallwas

PLAYTEST LENGTH:
15 Minutes

NOTES:

Stephen thinks that our HUD look great in theme but has problems with the opacity and size of the HUD. Stephen commented that our HUD looks like it takes up about 30% of our screen. Stephen did not immediately recognize the cooldown meter, the jump meter, and the pest counter. Stephen commented that the jump meter should probably go from being semi transparent to opaque and make a "ding" noise when players are allowed to jump to a new planet. Stephen also did not like the current weapon icon sprites (they are placeholder). Stehpen suggested that we supplement our weapon icon sprites with text to help players understand which weapon they currently have equipped. Stephen liked the style of the health meter and the shield meter but wants them to animate instead of just sliding down when the player is hit.

PLAYTESTER 3:
Michael Van Zant

PLAYTEST LENGTH:
15 Minutes

NOTES:

Michael did not have a problem understanding most aspects of the HUD. Michael did not understand immediately what the jump meter is or what it represents. Michael also thinks that the HUD is too white overall and suggested we lower the opacity of the HUD as a whole. Michael thought the aiming reticle is odd, and wants it to animate as he fires the gun. Michael understand the cooldown meter, but does not like the current design of it. Michael suggested having the cooldown meter under the reticle. Michael also wishes that our HUD was not so static and suggested a variety of screen shake, animations, and sfx that our HUD should have in order to really make the game come alive. Michael thought the current theme of the HUD looked good but had nothing exceptional to say about it other than it looked the part.
